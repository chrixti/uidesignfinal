What Can You Compost?

Click a card to find out!

Remember, effective composting also requires air and water to support the decomposition process.

Carbon-rich Materials (Browns)
  • Dry leaves
  • Plant stalks and twigs
  • Shredded paper (non-glossy, uncolored) and brown bags
  • Shredded cardboard (no wax coating, tape, or glue)
  • Untreated wood chips
Nitrogen-rich Materials (Greens)
  • Fruit and vegetable scraps
  • Grass clippings
  • Coffee grounds and paper filters
  • Paper tea bags (no staples)
  • Eggshells (crushed)

The Images Show Carbon to Nitrogen Ratios for Composting Materials:

  • "Green" Materials: Low C:N ratios (e.g., 5:1 to 25:1) indicating higher nitrogen content. This accelerates decomposition and supplies essential nitrogen to microbes.
  • "Brown" Materials: High C:N ratios (e.g., 35:1 to 400:1) indicating higher carbon content. This provides energy for the process but decomposes slowly.

Optimal Composting: Aim for an overall C:N ratio of about 25-30:1 by mixing greens and browns to ensure efficient decomposition and prevent issues like odors.
